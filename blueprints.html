Contract Templates

Settlement-safe contract templates

Reference library for the Rule Linter. The Linter screens individual rule text; these templates show longer-form examples by market type.

Templates derived from observed dispute and revision patterns. Each blueprint specifies the contract fields most often missing or ambiguous on the markets that ended up disputed: event definition, primary source, secondary source / fallback, edge cases, time zone, settlement timing, invalid/N/A rules, and worked examples.

These are not platform-issued specifications. They are research-grade templates documenting which fields, if specified ex ante, would have prevented the most common settlement disputes in the panel.

Design philosophy

Each numeric threshold below (4-hour custody, 12-hour incursion, battalion-strength, 100 paying customers) is a market-design choice, not legal truth. A different platform may pick different thresholds and still write a settlement-safe blueprint.

The thresholds are written narrowly to make the "did the event happen?" question answerable from a single named source within a fixed window. Wider thresholds (e.g. "any U.S. forces crossing the border" rather than "battalion-strength for ≥12 hours") are also valid, but require more elaborate edge-case handling.

If you use one of these on a platform, treat the body of the blueprint as a starting point. Adjust thresholds to the question your market is actually about, and document why you chose them. The seven-field structure (event / source / fallback / edge cases / time zone / settlement timing / invalid rules / examples) is what carries the settlement-safety guarantee. The specific thresholds are local choices.

Short version: the structure is universal, the thresholds are not.

The Blueprint template

Every blueprint below uses the same seven fields:

  1. Event definition. The world-state question, written to eliminate semantic ambiguity. Must specify scope, agent, action, object.
  2. Primary source. Named external authority. Never the platform itself. URL or registry.
  3. Secondary source / fallback. What happens if the primary source is unavailable, delayed, or itself disputed.
  4. Edge cases. Pre-specified handling for cancellation, postponement, ties, ambiguous timing, partial events.
  5. Time zone. Explicit zone for cutoff times. Avoid relative phrases ("today", "this week").
  6. Settlement timing. Window within which the resolution is evaluated after the event.
  7. Invalid / N/A rules. When the contract resolves Invalid (50/50) rather than Yes or No.

Blueprint 1 — U.S. government shutdown

Failure mode observed in panel: cutoff date inserted after trading began; "shutdown" defined inconsistently across markets.

Event definitionResolves YES if a federal funding lapse begins on or before {deadline} AT {time} {tz}, defined as the U.S. federal government failing to enact appropriations covering at least one of the 12 standard appropriations bills. The event triggers regardless of duration.
Primary sourceOffice of Management and Budget shutdown memo, OR OPM furlough guidance.
Secondary / fallbackIf neither OMB nor OPM publishes within 24h: Associated Press wire confirming federal agencies have begun furloughing non-essential employees.
Edge cases(a) Continuing Resolution passed after midnight but retroactive to midnight: NO. (b) Partial shutdown affecting one agency: YES. (c) Shutdown begins ≤30 minutes after deadline due to administrative delay: YES if a CR was not signed by 11:59pm {tz}. (d) Government open but selected agencies closed for unrelated reasons: NO.
Time zoneEastern Time. Cutoff is 11:59:59 PM ET on {deadline}.
Settlement timingResolves within 48 hours of the cutoff time.
Invalid / N/AResolves Invalid only if both primary and fallback sources are unavailable for > 7 days.

Blueprint 2 — Military ground incursion

Failure mode observed in panel: "ground incursion" / "invasion" defined too loosely; oracle vote can override widely-reported reality if rule is silent on territorial control.

Event definitionResolves YES if regular forces of {actor} physically cross the internationally-recognized border into {target territory} with at least one ground unit (battalion-strength or larger) and remain inside that territory for at least 12 hours, before {deadline}.
Primary sourceJoint statement from the actor's defense ministry AND the target government's defense ministry confirming the incursion. If only one confirms: secondary source applies.
Secondary / fallbackTwo of: Reuters, Associated Press, BBC News reporting confirmed ground presence. UN Secretary-General statement also counts.
Edge cases(a) Special-forces raid <12h: NO. (b) Air or naval action only: NO. (c) Local proxy forces, not regular military: NO. (d) Cross-border artillery without ground troops: NO. (e) Disputed border with active third-party claim: applies to the most recent UN-recognized line of control.
Time zoneUTC.
Settlement timingResolves within 7 days of confirmation.
Invalid / N/AResolves Invalid only if conflicting authoritative reports persist for > 14 days.

Blueprint 3 — "Will X be arrested by Y?"

Failure mode observed in panel: ambiguity over what counts as "arrest" (booking, indictment, custody, detention).

Event definitionResolves YES if {person} is taken into formal custody by {jurisdiction} law-enforcement personnel and held for at least one continuous period of 4 hours, before {deadline} {time} {tz}. Voluntary surrender to authorities counts. Issuance of an arrest warrant alone does not.
Primary sourceOfficial press release from the arresting agency (e.g., FBI, DOJ, foreign equivalent).
Secondary / fallbackTwo of: Reuters, AP, BBC reporting custody confirmed by the agency or by counsel.
Edge cases(a) Indictment without arrest: NO. (b) Brief detention <4h then released: NO. (c) Voluntary surrender at agency office: YES. (d) Arrest abroad but extradition pending: YES. (e) House arrest under formal court order: YES.
Time zone{tz} of arresting jurisdiction.
Settlement timingResolves within 48 hours of confirmed custody.
Invalid / N/AResolves Invalid only if no authoritative source confirms or denies within 14 days of deadline.

Blueprint 4 — "Will X launch product Y?"

Failure mode observed in panel: "launch" can mean announce, demo, beta, or generally available. Brand-vs-functional ambiguity (e.g., "robotaxi" vs general autonomous vehicle).

Event definitionResolves YES if {company} makes {product} commercially available to non-employees in {geography}, with paid usage by at least 100 distinct customers, before {deadline}. Announcements, demos, beta programs limited to employees, and waitlist openings do NOT count.
Primary sourceCompany SEC filing or press release stating commercial availability with a launch date.
Secondary / fallbackApp-store listing showing public availability + at least one independent media confirmation (Reuters / AP / Bloomberg / Wall Street Journal).
Edge cases(a) Beta with public access but waitlist: NO. (b) Available in select cities only: YES if any geography listed is covered. (c) Re-branded existing product: NO unless feature set materially changes per company release. (d) Launched then withdrawn within 7 days: still YES.
Time zoneUTC of company's primary listing exchange.
Settlement timingResolves within 14 days of confirmed launch.
Invalid / N/AResolves Invalid if the product is launched in name only with no working software / hardware delivered.

Blueprint 5 — Sports result

Sports contracts already settle relatively cleanly in the panel (37% revision rate among repeated-adapter chains is the main pattern, often from re-filing requests rather than ambiguous rules). This blueprint targets the residual pathology: cancellations, ties, and abandoned games.

Event definitionResolves YES if {team A} wins {event} as defined by the {official body}'s posted final result. A "win" requires meeting the body's definition (e.g., higher score after regulation + any tiebreaker).
Primary sourceOfficial body's posted final result page (URL).
Secondary / fallbackTwo of: ESPN, official broadcast partner, two major wire services.
Edge cases(a) Cancelled before start: Invalid. (b) Abandoned mid-game: rules of the body apply (typically counts if > X minutes played). (c) Tied per body rules: NO. (d) Team forfeits: opposing team wins. (e) Result reversed by appeal > 7 days after settlement: original settlement stands.
Time zoneUTC.
Settlement timingResolves within 24 hours of result posting.
Invalid / N/ACancellation; abandonment without applicable body rules.

How to use these

  1. Before listing a market, find the closest blueprint and copy the seven fields.
  2. Replace placeholders with the specific event ({deadline}, {actor}, {company}). Do not skip fields.
  3. Analyze similar historical contracts via Analyze to see whether the corresponding markets had revisions or candidate mismatches. If revision rates are high in this category × chain type, tighten the edge-case section.
  4. Publish the full spec as part of the market's resolution criteria — not just the summary question. Most disputes in the panel start because the resolution criteria field is shorter than this blueprint.

These blueprints are research output, not platform-issued specs. They are released so platforms, journalists, and traders can compare a proposed contract against a settlement-safer version derived from observed dispute patterns. Research → documents the dispute panel that grounds these patterns.
