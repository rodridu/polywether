Core definitions

A market price is only useful if the contract says clearly what counts as YES, NO, or INVALID. These are the terms the rest of the site uses.

Settlement risk
Risk that a market's payoff depends on ambiguous resolution rules, weak sources, or dispute mechanics — not only on the real-world event.
Disputed contract
A Polymarket / UMA contract where at least one counter-bond was posted (n=2,221 in the panel).
Settlement revision
The final payoff differs from the first proposed outcome.
Chain type
The path the contract follows through UMA resolution. Two main types: request-voiding (DVM voids a request, adapter re-files) and repeated adapter-routed request (multiple requests against the same contract without DVM voiding).
Candidate mismatch
Diagnostic flag: the final payoff appears to disagree with a rule-implied label coded from the recoverable contract text. Not a proven error.
Risk tier
Screening label (High / Caution / Low) based on rule clarity and historical settlement patterns. Not a prediction that settlement will be wrong.

Liquidity-style monitors (e.g. Bellwether) ask whether a price is robust enough to cite. This monitor asks a different question: is the contract clean enough that the price means what users think it means?
