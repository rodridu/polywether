Why settlement risk is its own layer

Prediction-market prices are usually read as beliefs about events. But traders are paid on settlement labels, not on world events. Three objects can disagree:

  • World facts. What actually happened.
  • Rule-implied label. What the contract's text and source hierarchy would say.
  • Settlement state. The label the mechanism actually delivers as the payoff.

Liquidity-style monitors (e.g. Bellwether) ask whether a price is robust enough to cite. This monitor asks a different question: is the contract clean enough that the price means what users think it means?
