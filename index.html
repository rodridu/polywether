What this explorer measures

The final outcome of a prediction-market contract is not a primitive variable. It is produced by a settlement process whose internal steps can be observed. This project distinguishes three objects:

  • World facts. What actually happened.
  • Rule-implied label. What the contract's text and source hierarchy would say.
  • Settlement state. The label the mechanism actually delivers as the payoff.

These three objects can coincide, but they need not. Settlement is part of prediction-market microstructure, not merely a back-office recording step.

The settlement funnel

Of 661,949 settlement conditions, 2,221 are disputed. Of the 2,197 disputed contracts with an observed final payoff, 530 (24.1%) end with a final payoff different from the first proposed outcome.

Where revisions come from

A "request-voiding chain" is one in which UMA's Data Verification Mechanism voids the request on a prior episode, forcing the adapter to re-file. A "repeated adapter-routed request" is one in which the adapter files multiple requests against the same contract without any DVM voiding.
