About

Data, scope, limitations

Public-facing companion to the working paper "Settlement-State Construction in Prediction-Market Contracts." The Funnel page carries the framing argument; this page documents how the data are constructed.

Data construction

Polymarket markets and UMA Optimistic Oracle resolution events are ingested into a research database. The settlement panel covers all Polymarket conditions resolved via UMA (n=661,949). The disputed-contract sample (D=1, n=2,221) is the union of contracts with at least one counter-bond posted. Episode-level chains are reconstructed from the UMA RequestPrice / DisputePrice / SettlePrice event sequence.

Question text comes from a COALESCE over pm_markets.question, episode_panel.question_text, and pm_resolution_events.ancillary_data_decoded. Description (resolution criteria) comes from pm_markets.raw.description. Category comes from episode_panel.category.

The candidate-mismatch layer uses a two-pass LLM coding protocol against the recoverable contract text and named source. 94 candidate mismatches are identified; 64 are agreed across both coding passes (category-level aggregate; per-row agreement is not exposed).

What we do NOT claim

  • The unconditional rate at which a randomly selected market receives an erroneous final payoff.
  • Whether any specific contract's final payoff is correct in real-world terms. (Locator pages do not adjudicate.)
  • UMA tokenholder voter identity, vote concentration, or turnout.
  • Causal effects of any UMA policy change.

Limitations

  • Question-text coverage is 79.1%. 1,756 of 2,221 disputed contracts have recoverable question text. The other 465 are visible in the rev3 settlement panel but absent from Polymarket-side metadata (likely Kalshi-only or deprecated condition_ids). The Contract Explorer shows [question text missing] for affected rows; chain type and final payoff are still observed.
  • Auditability flags 4–6 (named source / fallback / edge cases) are heuristic regex over a noisy text corpus. They tag whether the relevant language appears, not whether the contract was correctly designed.
  • Public Case Locator uses keyword matching on pm_markets.question. Three of seven curated cases (Venezuela / Iran-Khamenei / Lebanon) match in the market panel but not in the disputed-analysis sample — could mean the relevant contract was excluded under the paper's sample-construction rules, or the public dispute attached to a Kalshi-side market not visible here.

Refresh cadence

Aggregate JSONs (funnel, chain signatures, candidate mismatches) regenerate weekly via a GitHub Actions workflow that reads small CSVs in data-source/. Per-contract data and the duckdb-wasm DB are refreshed manually when the paper's analysis pipeline runs. See the repo README for the refresh workflow.

Reading the paper

The full working paper, "Settlement-State Construction in Prediction-Market Contracts," contains the formal estimands, sample-construction details, robustness checks, and full case appendices. The dashboard is intentionally a thinner artifact — you can see the funnel and chain mechanics here, but the academic argument lives in the paper.
